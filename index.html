W H Y ?

I saw a couple articles about facial recognition in Python and I wanted to see if this data-science-y technology could be easily adapted to the front-end of a project. I'm also interested in the intersection of art and code, and virtual reality seems like a really interesting medium for that.

H O W ?

I found a bunch of different libraries that allow you to implement facial recognition in JavaScript -- including clmtracker, ccv, headtrackr, and Tracking.js. The documentation was best for Tracking.js, so I went with that.

T H E    M A T H

Tracking.js uses the Viola Jones: an algorithm for facial detection. The algorithm focuses on a few facial features, for example:

  • The eye region is darker than the upper-cheeks.
  • The nose bridge region is brighter than the eyes.

The algorithm adds up the pixel values in a rectangle and compares them to the sum of the pixels in another rectangle in the image. Light rectangles are positive and dark ones are negative. It uses those differences to classify whether or not a rectangle contains a face.
